1. **The auth-fork is background-initiated, and that path now works.** The first attempt failed because we navigated to a bare `account.proton.me` — but the fork is driven by the *extension*: on `runtime.onInstalled` (which a real browser fires and we did not), `background.js` calls `tabs.create` to open `account.proton.me/pass/extension-onboarding?loginParams=app=proton-pass-extension&state=<nonce>`. Fixed by: firing `runtime.onInstalled` on first boot, and wiring `tabs.create`/`windows.create` to drive the shell's tab. The onboarding page now loads with the correct fork params. (Verified headlessly: `OPEN-URL create -> account.proton.me/pass/extension-onboarding`.)

3. **Shell + injection + isolation** all functioning in the real tab.

## The blocker reached

On the onboarding page, Proton's account app renders:

> **"Proton Pass is missing permissions — Please open your browser extension settings and allow Proton Pass extension to access website data. Then, reload this page."**

So the account app **detects the extension but concludes it lacks host access** to the page — the extension↔page permission/presence handshake is not satisfied.

## Leading hypothesis + next step: inject `orchestrator.js` (E5)

`manifest.json` declares **three** content scripts, on `account.proton.me`:
- `orchestrator.js` — general, **all** http(s) pages, isolated world
- `fork.js` — account.proton.me only, isolated world
- `webauthn.js` — all pages, **MAIN** world

**We inject only `fork.js` + our content-shim — not `orchestrator.js` (nor `webauthn.js`).** `orchestrator.js` is the general content script that establishes the extension↔page bridge and signals presence/permission; the account app's detection almost certainly depends on it. This is the prime suspect for "missing permissions."

**Implication — a resequencing finding:** injecting `orchestrator.js` is E5's general-injection + frame-registry work (FR-9). So **E6's completion is coupled to E5**; the roadmap sequenced E6 before E5, but the login handshake needs the general content-script coverage E5 provides. Recommend: do E5 (or at least `orchestrator.js` injection) before finishing the E6 gate.

**Not certainty:** the "missing permissions" mechanism should be confirmed (does the account app detect via `orchestrator.js` specifically, via a `permissions.contains` probe, or another bridge?). A cheap experiment: inject `orchestrator.js` into the page's isolated world the same way as `fork.js` and re-check whether the error clears — before committing to full E5.
